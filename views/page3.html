Card Reveal

Card Reveal is an effect that consists of content, mostly of an image, that reveals more information once clicked with optional icons on the bottom right to click as well. Inside the div.card, add the three other divs: card-image, card-content (description before reveal) and card-reveal (description after reveal). Just add the card-reveal div with a span.card-title inside to make this work. Add the class activator to the image and content inside the card to allow it to open the card reveal. Scroll to the next tutorial to see it in action.

HTML

<div class="card"> <div class="card-image kitchen"> <img class="activator" src="/public/img/kitchen.jpg"> </div> <div class="card-content"> <span class="card-title activator">Card Title <i class="material-icons right">more_vert</i></span> <p>Add text (optional)</p> </div> <div class="card-reveal"> <span class="card-title">Card Title <i class="material-icons right">close</i></span> <p>Add text</p> </div> </div>

Material Box

When a user clicks on an image that can be enlarged,Material box centers the image and enlarges it in a smooth, non-jarring manner. To dismiss the image, the user can either click on the image again, scroll away, or press the ESC key. Click the image below to see it in action.

HTML

<img class="materialboxed" width="650" src="/public/img/kengowalk.jpg">

JavaScript

$(document).ready(function(){ $('.materialboxed').materialbox(); });

Tooltips

Tooltips are small, interactive, textual hints for mainly graphical elements. Hover over a tooltip to gain more information on the text, button or other element it is used for.
data-position dictates what position (left, right, top or bottom) the tooltip appears. data-tooltip sets the text in the tooltip

HTML

<a class="btn tooltipped" data-position="top" data-tooltip="I am a tooltip">Button Text</a>

JavaScript

$(document).ready(function(){ $('.tooltipped').tooltip(); });

Slider

A simple and elegant image slideshow with transitions. You can also have captions that will be transitioned on their own depending on their alignment. You can also have indicators that show up on the bottom of the slider. This feature is only shown in mobile or tablet version for demo page

HTML

<div class="slider"> <ul class="slides"> <li> <img src="yourimage.jpg"> <div class="caption center-align"> <h3>This is our big Tagline!</h3> <h5 class="light grey-text text-lighten-3">Here's our small slogan.</h5> </div> </li> <li> <img src="yourimage.jpg"> <div class="caption left-align"> <h3>Left Aligned Caption</h3> <h5 class="light grey-text text-lighten-3">Here's our small slogan.</h5> </div> </li>

JavaScript

$(document).ready(function(){ $('.slider').slider(); });
